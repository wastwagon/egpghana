Audit notice — why domain and hosting renewals are arranged through the developer (OceanCyber)

EGP is billed for registrar and hosting costs that OceanCyber pays directly to third-party providers (Namecheap, Hostinger). Renewals are currently processed through the developer rather than by EGP staff for the following documented reasons:

  • Service continuity: The live platform (egpghana.org) was deployed and is operated under technical accounts established during development. Immediate transfer of registrar and hosting control without coordinated migration would risk downtime, loss of SSL/DNS configuration, or interrupted public access.
  • Technical configuration: Domain DNS, VPS hosting (Coolify), database, backups, and security settings are integrated. Renewal and hosting changes require specialist configuration that the developer currently maintains on EGP’s behalf.
  • Institutional transition in progress: Per the EGP team meeting, hosting and related services are being transferred to an official EGP email account and institutional ownership. Until that handover is complete, the developer continues renewals so the organisation’s digital presence is not disrupted.
  • Audit and reimbursement: This invoice itemises third-party costs plus tax so EGP can reimburse documented expenditure. Receipts from registrars and Hostinger can be provided on request. Ownership transfer to EGP accounts remains the agreed next step once institutional billing and access are in place.
